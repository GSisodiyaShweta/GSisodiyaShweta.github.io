About Me

I'm a Computational Media Ph.D. student in the Interaction Dynamics Lab at UC Santa Cruz. I earned my bachelor's and master's in Applied Mathematics from the Indian Insitute of Technology Roorkee (IITR, India) in 2018. I served as Data Science Analyst (full-time) at Accenture (India) for three years before joining UC Santa Cruz. I am primarily interested in user experience, game user research, and Interactive Narrative. Currently, my focus is player agency and visualizing it in the context of game narrative. Much of the motivation is to gain insights into how player agency allows players to make different choices compared to other players or communities.


Selected Projects

Shibu

There exists a spectrum of gender identities in this world. Fostering gender-biased communication, regardless of setting, can ingrain unconscious biases. Thus, as a community we should promote Gender-Inclusive Language (GIL) as a good social practice.As a community we have begun making small but measurable efforts to recognize the gender — instituting GIL in communications. At present community introduces GIL via seminars, workshops, blog posts, videos, animations, etc. Undoubtedly, such platforms are excellent for abstract conceptualization. However, when it comes to communication, such platforms are mere warm-ups. There is a need for an interactive platform that encourages individuals to learn and practice GIL holistically.

To highlight this gap between insitutionalization and implementation of GIL, we present SHIBU. Research suggest that the first step towards adopting more Gender-Inclusive Language is simply bringing awareness around this topic and getting familiar with pronouns. Shibu is an interactive chatbot game, that highlights the inappropriate use of gender pronouns.

Project Resilience

Resilience, the ability of a person to manage their own anxiety and cope with a changing environment, is a key psychological construct that is becoming increasingly salient in light of the COVID-19 pandemic, extreme weather events and political and social unrest. Previous work in psychology and organizational psychology focused on measuring resilience as a trait and determining factors that affect it.Addtionally previous work is limited methodologically to surveys, which rely on self-report and do not measure such dynamic aspects of resilience. Our novel approach uses Alternate Reality Games (ARGs), to address this research gap.
